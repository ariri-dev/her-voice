Kalpana Chawla

Introduction

Kalpana Chawla was the first Indian-born woman to go to space. She was a brave astronaut and an inspiration to millions of people around the world.

Early Life

  • Full Name: Kalpana Chawla
  • Born: 17 March 1962
  • Birthplace: Karnal, Haryana, India

Education

  • Studied aeronautical engineering in India
  • Completed higher studies in the United States

Career

  • Became an astronaut at NASA
  • First space mission in 1997
  • Flew on Space Shuttle Columbia

Major Achievements

  • First Indian-born woman in space
  • Participated in important space research missions
  • Inspired many students to pursue science and space careers

Tragedy

  • Died in the Space Shuttle Columbia disaster on 1 February 2003

Conclusion

Kalpana Chawla will always be remembered as a symbol of courage, determination, and dedication to science.
